Who We Are

We see our management, staff, students, host employers and other stakeholders as part of the Mathosim Trimming and Upholstery family and encourage creativity and innovation. Achievements from both staff and students are recognized and rewarded and together we: EQUIP, EMPOWER AND EXCEL!

Our range of programmes is based on the list of critical and scarce skills as identified by the South African Government, but at the same time we are constantly seeking to improve and expand on these programmes to ensure we stay current and even ahead of the ever-changing needs in the industry. We believe that the aforementioned will undoubtedly foster job creation, especially by means of apprenticeships, learnerships and skills programmes incorporated into work placement. Through skills development constant job creation takes place. As more bursaries are awarded, more learners are recruited, which in turn leads to more facilitators to be employed. Learners recruited in our programmes we placed them at Host Employers which in turn benefits the business community and offering students the unique opportunity to work and gain the necessary experience while in training.

Our Objectives

  • The need to understand the environment in terms of the future and planning influences

Our Values

  • We encourage creativity and value adding initiatives from both internal and clients are considerable in doing so.
  • We are committed in investing time and money in our business continuously train and develop our internal clients to ensure high level of efficiency.
  • We reward, recognize and promote on basis of performance and merits based on a sound performance measurement tool.
  • We are passionate about excellence customer services both internally and externally are obsessed about delivering beyond expectations.
  • We are committed to a stress-free working environment, which will enhance individual well-being and thus increase productivity.
  • We are profit driven organization which invest in systems and programs to reduce costs compromising the delivery of high-quality products and services.
